Classical RAG vs Graph-RAG

The core difference is not the LLM, but how external knowledge is represented and retrieved.

User Question political, temporal, or multi-hop analysis
Classical RAG
Retrieves text passages that are semantically close to the query.
1

Represent corpus as chunks

Documents are split and embedded into a vector index.

2

Retrieve similar passages

The system returns top-k chunks based mainly on semantic similarity.

3

Generate from retrieved context

The answer is grounded in passages, but relationships may remain implicit.

Graph-RAG
Retrieves evidence through entities, relations, communities, and text.
1

Represent corpus as a graph

Entities, events, claims, and relations are extracted from the documents.

2

Traverse relational evidence

Retrieval can follow links across actors, places, events, and time periods.

3

Generate with structured grounding

The answer can combine local evidence with broader graph context.

When Graph-RAG helps Classical RAG is strong for direct evidence lookup. Graph-RAG is better suited to geopolitical questions that require entity disambiguation, relation traversal, provenance, and multi-hop reasoning.
